Key Players

John Hannah was the twelfth president at Michigan State University and held the position from 1941 to 1969. During his tenure,
with the help of the G.I. bill, Hannah helped MSU become a leading research institution. Hannah become the head of the
United States Agency for of International Development after he departure from the University.

George Axinn joined MSU in 1953 as Extension editor, and later was promoted to Associate Director of Extension in 1955.
He served in this role until 1960, and during that time he created the Institute for Extension Personnel Development. It is
through the Institute that became coordinator for the MSU-Nigeria Program.

Joseph Druse joined MSU in 1954 as an instructor in Humanities. Through his capacity as a History and Humanities
professor, Druse served a two-year assignment at the University of Nigeria from 1962-1964.

Charles “Bud” Doane Jr. served as part of MSU’s International Program Staff, assisting in the establishment of MSU
presence in Nigeria, Pakistan, and Nepal. He traveled to Nigeria many times in the early 1960s to aid in the development
of the University. Bud was later appointed Director of the Office of Overseas Support at MSU.

Dr. Kenneth Dike was a Nigeria educator and activist, serving as the first Vice-Chancellor of the University of
Ibadan from 1962 to 1967. He also served as the Chairman of Antiquities in Nigeria in the 1960s.
